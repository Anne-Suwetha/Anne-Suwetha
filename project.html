The Jaffna Heritage Route is a cultural and historical journey through the northern part of Sri Lanka, particularly the Jaffna Peninsula. This area is rich in Tamil culture, history, and traditions, offering a unique perspective on the diverse heritage of the island.

Highlights:

Jaffna Fort: Built by the Portuguese in the 17th century and later fortified by the Dutch, this well-preserved fort offers insight into colonial architecture and history.

Nallur Kandaswamy Temple: One of the most important Hindu temples in Sri Lanka, dedicated to Lord Murugan.

Jaffna Library: Once one of the largest libraries in Asia, it was rebuilt after being destroyed in the civil conflict.
